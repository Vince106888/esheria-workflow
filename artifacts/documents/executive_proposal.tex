\kicker{// Executive Summary}
{\headingfont\bfseries\Huge\color{Navy} The Platform Shift}

\tealrule

Esheria currently operates five strong legal AI capabilities: document OCR and ingestion, contract
compliance analysis, legal research and retrieval, AI-assisted drafting, and analytics
infrastructure. Each operates as a credible, functioning service. However, they share no data
model, no workflow state, and no orchestration layer—making them five separate products rather
than one coherent platform.

\medskip

This proposal argues that Esheria's most significant near-term strategic opportunity is not to add
a sixth capability, but to build the orchestration layer above the five that already exist: a
\textbf{Legal Workflow Operating System} with shared canonical objects, workflow state machines,
human-in-loop review, approval chains, and institutional analytics.

\bigskip

\begin{tealbox}{// KEY INSIGHT}
Esheria's strongest long-term moat comes from becoming the programmable operating layer through
which organisations run intelligent legal workflows—not from accumulating isolated AI features.
Platform businesses compound. Feature businesses plateau.
\end{tealbox}

\thinrule

%% ════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
%%  SECTION 01: THE PROBLEM
%% ════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
\sectionbanner{01}{The Strategic Problem}{Why five capabilities without a platform creates a growth ceiling}{Teal}

\subsection{Current State Diagnosis}

The current state across Esheria's ecosystem shows clear signs of capability fragmentation.
Authentication and identity models differ across product surfaces. There is no shared canonical
representation of Documents, Matters, or Clauses. Workflow state is not preserved between services.
Analytics serves Esheria's internal operations but not customer organisations. There are no
approval chains, escalation paths, or SLA enforcement mechanisms.

\subsection{What Enterprise Buyers Actually Need}

B2B enterprise buyers in legal, compliance, procurement, and financial services do not buy
isolated AI features indefinitely. Their buying criteria are fundamentally different from
individual user adoption:

\bigskip

\rowcolors{2}{Light}{white}
\begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{%
  >{\headingfont\bfseries\small\color{Text}}L{72mm}%
  >{\small\color{TextLt}}X%
}
\rowcolor{Navy2}
\thead{Enterprise Requirement} & \thead{Esheria Current Position} \\
\midrule
Repeatable, auditable workflows       & Features without shared workflow state \\
Role-based access and controls        & Varies by product surface \\
DMS, CRM, ERP integrations            & Not yet systematised across services \\
Approval chains and escalation        & Not present as platform primitives \\
Institution-facing analytics          & Internal to Esheria operations only \\
Evidence-backed AI outputs            & Output-only, not linked to citation source \\
Compliance and audit trails           & Not a first-class architectural concern \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}

\bigskip

\begin{amberbox}{// THE ENTERPRISE WORKFLOW IMPERATIVE}
Upload a contract. Analyse it. Flag risks. Draft corrections. Route for review.
Obtain approval. Close the matter. Generate a compliance report.
That is one workflow. That is what enterprise legal teams will pay for at scale.
\end{amberbox}

\thinrule

%%  SECTION 02: PLATFORM THESIS
%% ════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
\sectionbanner{02}{The Platform Thesis}{Orchestration-first integration with governance, tenancy, and audit}{Teal}

\subsection{From Features to Operating Layer}

The core thesis is direct: Esheria should evolve from a collection of legal AI capabilities into
a \textbf{programmable legal operations platform}—the operating layer through which organisations
run intelligent legal workflows. This is a category shift, not merely a product expansion. The
five existing repos become Layer~1 of a five-layer architecture. They are not rebuilt—they are
connected.

\subsection{Platform Capabilities}

%% ════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
\sectionbanner{03}{Platform Architecture}{Five-layer stack — existing services connected, not rebuilt}{Blue}

The platform is structured as five horizontal layers. Each has a distinct purpose and a bounded
responsibility. No layer has a direct dependency on a non-adjacent layer. Existing capability
repos plug into Layer~1 as-is.

\bigskip

\begin{amberbox}{// CRITICAL ARCHITECTURAL DECISION}
Define and lock the Canonical Object Model (Layer~3) before writing any service code.
The schema is the contract between all services, operators, and frontends.
Treat schema changes within a major version with the same rigour as a public API breaking change.
\end{amberbox}
